Curation policy

This page explains how papers are selected for the site.

Prefer strong ophthalmic relevance

Foundation-model papers with direct retinal or ophthalmic scope come first.

Use broader inclusion where needed

Visual grounding and hyperbolic methods may temporarily include medically transferable work when ophthalmic-native papers are still sparse.

Original links over secondary summaries

Official sources are preferred whenever available.

Stay explicit about uncertainty

If venue or publication status is not fully verified, the note should say so instead of pretending certainty.

Clear curation rules keep the paper hub trustworthy.
